The Dhiaan Dave Manifesto

I believe the best way to learn is to build things that don't exist yet.

I didn't grow up in a family of engineers. I grew up with curiosity and a laptop. That was enough. I learned to code because I wanted to make games, then websites, then circuits, then robots. Every project taught me more than any class could.

I organize hackathons because community accelerates growth. When fifty people sit in a room building things they care about, magic happens. Someone's first line of Python turns into a working prototype by morning. That transformation is what keeps me going.

Build first. Ask for permission later.

On Education

School teaches you what's known. Building teaches you what's possible. I value both, but I prioritize the latter. A textbook can explain Ohm's law. A soldering iron teaches you why it matters.

On Technology

Hardware and software are not separate worlds. A PCB is just code for electrons. A program is just a circuit for data. I build at the intersection because that's where the interesting problems live.

"The best way to predict the future is to build it."

On Community

I've seen what happens when you give young people the tools and the space to create. They build things that shock you. Telos Tutoring exists because education should be accessible. Cove Hacks exists because opportunity should be everywhere, not just in Silicon Valley boardrooms.

On Growth

I'm 14, and I have a lot to learn. That's not a weakness, it's an advantage. I haven't been told what's impossible yet. Every setback is just data. Every failure is a pull request to my own understanding.

I'm looking for unpaid Summer 2026 internships not because I need the resume, but because I want to work alongside people who build things at a level I haven't reached yet. I learn fastest when I'm the least experienced person in the room.

What I Stand For

Accessibility — Technology should empower everyone, not just those who can afford it. Telos Tutoring is free by design.

Craft — Details matter. The difference between good and great is the willingness to polish.

Curiosity — Ask why, then ask how, then build the answer.

Community over competition — The rising tide lifts all boats. Share what you learn.

This is my philosophy. It will evolve as I do. But the core will stay the same: build things, help people, stay curious.

Dhiaan Dave — Milpitas, California — 2026
